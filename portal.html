How Glasi works
A short read.

What Glasi is

Glasi is an on-demand staffing platform for bars. When a venue gets slammed, they request a Glasi from us and we dispatch a vetted local worker within 30 minutes.

You're the local worker. You sign up, get vetted, get assigned a zone, and join sessions on Friday and Saturday evenings (plus the occasional weekday). When a shift comes in during a session, the workers in that session get the chance to respond. We pick one and dispatch.

How shifts work

You join a session from home. You're not paid for waiting. You're only paid when you're selected for and complete a shift.

If a shift comes in, you'll see it on this app with a 60-second response window. Tap "I'm available" if you're ready to go. We pick a worker and dispatch them. If it's you, you'll see venue details, get a clock-in code, head to the bar, and clock in.

You work the shift, get a clock-out code from the manager, clock out, and you're done.

How pay works

You're paid via Parasol Group, our umbrella payroll partner. Glasi sends them your timesheet, settles the invoice, and they pay you directly.

Parasol runs five payrolls a day. We aim to settle by their 3pm cutoff so you get paid the same evening.

How to be successful

Show up to sessions, even when it's quiet. We notice consistency. Respond fast when shifts come in. Arrive on time or early. Leave a good impression at every venue.

Your tier (and your hourly rate) goes up with points. Points come from showing up, responding, completing shifts, and arriving on time. The Tiers tab has the full breakdown.

How selection works
When a shift comes in, here's what affects your chances.

The 60-second window

When a bar requests a Glasi, every worker in the live session sees the shift post for 60 seconds. Tap "I'm available" if you can take it. Tap "I'll pass" if you can't. After 60 seconds the window closes and we pick.

You can only respond once. Speed matters — fast responses earn more points and signal to us that you're ready.

What gets you picked

We pick based on a mix of:

Tier. Higher-tier workers get a small edge. Tier reflects your track record.

Reliability. Your attendance percentage on shifts you were selected for. Show up consistently and this stays high.

Response speed. Replied within the first 25% of your personal window? That earns the quick-response bonus.

Distance. Closer to the venue means you'll arrive faster. We prefer workers within walking distance.

Transport. Workers with a bike, e-bike, or e-scooter cover ground faster. Mention it in your profile if you have one.

Fairness. If you've been responding all night and not getting picked, your odds tick up to balance things out.

Tips

Stay near a charger so your phone doesn't die mid-session.

Keep notifications on.

If you can't take a shift, tap "I'll pass" rather than not responding. It still earns you a small point bump and lets us know.
