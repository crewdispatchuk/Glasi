Information for Glasi workers

This page is for current and prospective Glasi workers. It explains who Glasi is, how the relationship between you, Glasi, and our employment partner Numbermill works, what your pay looks like, your rights as an agency worker, and where to go if you need help.

About Glasi

Glasi Ltd is a UK employment business based in Southampton. We supply temporary floor support workers to bars, pubs, and hospitality venues during busy periods.

We are registered in England and Wales (company number 17147716) and classified under SIC code 78200 (Temporary employment agency activities). Our registered office is at 63 Winchester Street, Botley, Southampton SO30 2EB.

We currently operate in Southampton, with planned expansion to Portsmouth and Winchester.

How working with Glasi works

Three parties are involved in every shift:

Glasi finds you assignments and operates the platform. We recruit you, vet you, schedule operational sessions, and match you with bars that need floor support.

Numbermill Ltd is your employer. They handle your employment contract, weekly pay, PAYE tax, National Insurance, holiday pay, statutory rights, and right-to-work checks. They are an established UK Professional Employer Organisation.

The bar is the hirer. They direct your work during a shift (telling you where to collect glasses, what areas need attention) but they are not your employer. They pay Glasi for your time. Glasi pays Numbermill. Numbermill pays you.

You apply through Glasi. Glasi passes you to Numbermill, who onboard you as their employee (right-to-work check, employment contract, identity verification). Once cleared, you use the Glasi app to receive shift opportunities at bars in your area.

You decide which shifts to accept. There is no minimum number of shifts. You can pass on every shift offered and remain a Glasi worker in good standing. Your employment with Numbermill continues regardless of whether you accept any specific shift through Glasi.

Your pay

You are paid weekly by Numbermill.

Glasi operates a five-tier pay structure. New workers start at Tier 1. As you build experience and reliability on the platform, you progress through the tiers and your hourly rate increases.

Tier 1 — £12.71 per hour

Tier 2 — £13.71 per hour

Tier 3 — £14.71 per hour

Tier 4 — £15.71 per hour

Tier 5 — £16.71 per hour

All rates are gross pay, before tax and National Insurance.

Before you complete your application with Glasi, you will see a Key Information Document that explains the full breakdown of deductions, holiday pay, and how the umbrella arrangement works.

Holiday pay is calculated at 12.07% of your gross wage. You can choose to accrue holiday pay (so it builds up and is paid when you take time off) or have it advanced weekly with your pay.

Your rights as an agency worker

As a Numbermill employee, you have full UK employment rights:

  • A written employment contract from Numbermill.
  • The right to be paid at least the National Minimum Wage or National Living Wage.
  • Paid statutory holiday entitlement.
  • Statutory sick pay where you qualify.
  • Auto-enrolment into a workplace pension (subject to eligibility).
  • Protection from unlawful discrimination under the Equality Act 2010.

As an agency worker under the Agency Workers Regulations 2010, you have additional rights:

  • From day one of any assignment at a bar, access to on-site facilities and amenities (canteens, toilets, drinking water, rest areas) on the same basis as bar staff.
  • From day one, information about employment opportunities at the bar.
  • After 12 continuous weeks performing the same role at the same bar, the right to basic working conditions equivalent to comparable workers employed directly by that bar.

Health and safety

The bar is responsible for the safety of its premises and for providing a safe place for you to work.

Before you can receive shift requests from a particular bar, you must read and agree to that bar's venue-specific health and safety documentation and walkthrough. This is available in your Glasi app. You only need to do this once per bar.

If you are ever asked to do something at a bar that falls outside the floor support tasks Glasi supplies workers for (for example, pulling pints, handling cash, kitchen work, door security, cleaning toilets, or heavy lifting), you have the right to decline. Your pay is not affected by declining out-of-scope tasks.

If you have a health and safety concern during a shift, raise it with the bar manager and report it to Glasi at hello@glasi.co.uk as soon as you can.
