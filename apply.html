How Glasi Works

Glasi connects busy bars around Southampton with reliable local people when they need extra hands at short notice. This is temporary bar support work, paid hourly. The work is simple. Glass collecting, clearing tables, and keeping the floor tidy. No till work, no stocking, nothing that needs training. Just wear plain black clothing, be ready to move, and show up with a good attitude.

What happens after you apply

We'll review your application. If we think you're suitable, we may follow up by phone or email with a few more questions. From there, if you're a fit for the bars we work with, we'll pass your information to our partner NumberMill Ltd.

NumberMill Ltd is your employer and runs your payroll. They handle standard right-to-work checks and get you on their payroll. More information about NumberMill is in the Key Information Document at the end of this form.

Once you're onboarded with NumberMill, Glasi will contact you to confirm your app login and send you a link to download the Glasi app. The app is how everything happens.

Sessions

A session is a predicted busy time for bars in your area. When a session is running, you join it through the app. That's how you become available for shifts.

Joining a session doesn't mean you're guaranteed a shift, but it is how you view live requests. Responding quickly to requests gives you the best chance of getting a shift.

Bars who request a worker during a session are always a maximum of 25 minutes walking distance from you.

When a bar requests a worker

During a session, you'll receive a push notification when a bar nearby requests a worker. Open the request and reply either "I'm available" or "I'll pass."

If you respond "I'm available," you're in the running for the shift. Our system picks the best worker for the job based on tier, reliability, response speed and distance to the venue. Responding fast gives you the best chance of being picked.

You'll know clearly in the app whether you got the shift. Only leave for the venue once you've been dispatched.

Heading to the venue

Once you're dispatched, the app will show you the venue and who to ask for when you arrive. Speed matters. The bar needs you fast.

How you get there is up to you, but walking, cycling, or e-scooter is best. Don't rely on public transport or anything that involves parking. No-shows are a serious problem.

At the venue

When you arrive, find the named contact shown in your app and tell them you're from Glasi. They'll give you a 4-digit clock-in code. Enter it in the app to start the shift.

Work hard, have fun, and enjoy it. When your shift ends, the bar manager will give you a clock-out code. Enter it in the app to complete the shift. After clocking out, you'll get feedback from the bar, and you can leave feedback on the bar too.

Why sessions still earn you something even when you don't get the shift

We wish we could dispatch everyone in a session, but we can't send everyone to every shift. Don't worry. Being in the session still rewards you.

You earn points for joining sessions, responding to requests (passing counts as responding, not responding doesn't), completing shifts, and arriving on time. Points build your tier. Your tier is your rate of pay.

Glasi has five tiers and each one pays £1 more per hour. Top workers earn up to £16.71 per hour for the same work. The more sessions you join and the more responsive you are, the higher your tier, which means a higher rate of pay every time you do get a shift.

You can also earn 50 points before your first shift by following @glasi_official on Instagram. Points carry over.

Pay

NumberMill Ltd pays you for every shift you complete. They're your employer and run your payroll. You're not paid for time spent in sessions. Sessions earn you points, which build your tier and unlock higher pay. NumberMill processes payroll weekly. You are paid on the Wednesday following your work week.

We've got your back

We only work with well-run venues that want you there. If anything feels off during a shift, contact us straight away. We're always on your side.
